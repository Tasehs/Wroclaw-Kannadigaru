Kannadigas in Wrocław 🇵🇱

A practical relocation guide for individuals and families from Karnataka

👋 Welcome

Moving to a new country is exciting, but it can also feel overwhelming — especially when leaving behind familiar language, culture, and community. This site is a friendly guide created for Kannada-speaking individuals and families settling in Wrocław, Poland. Whether you are arriving alone for work or relocating with family, the goal is to provide clear, helpful information — from visas and housing to healthcare and local support — so that you can feel confident, stay connected, and start building a sense of home, even when far from it

📦 Relocating Alone

  • Visa & Work Permit: Usually arranged by employer (national visa D or EU Blue Card)
  • Temporary Stay: Stay in hotels or short-term Airbnb until permanent flat is found
  • Housing: Find flats on OLX or Otodom
  • PESEL Number: Not mandatory, but useful for tax and healthcare
  • Health Insurance: Public (ZUS) or private providers like LuxMed / Medicover
  • Public Transport: Monthly pass ~110 PLN (JakDojade)

👨‍👩‍👧 Relocating with Family

  • Dependent Visas: Apply for spouse and children under family reunification
  • Kindergartens & Schools: Public (free, Polish-speaking) or private international schools (~1500–3000 PLN/month)
  • Language: Learn basic Polish; weekend classes are helpful
  • Family Health Insurance: Include dependents in your plan or buy private policies
  • Local Community: Join Kannada/Indian WhatsApp, Telegram, or Facebook groups

📝 Legal Formalities

  • Register address at Urząd Miasta (City Office) within 30 days
  • Apply for Karta Pobytu (residence card) if staying more than 3 months
  • Tax registration—check with HR or consult an accountant
  • Open a bank account with your passport + address proof
